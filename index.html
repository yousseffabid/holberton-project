My opinion on photography

“A picture is worth a thousand words.”
You might have heard it more than a thousand times in your life, but it’s very true! A picture captures time, but not just time. It captures the moment, the emotion, and everything in between.

Photography has always been a medium for informing, delighting and grabbing attention. It showcases the beautiful side of the world as well as the ugly side. It portrays everything without uttering a word. It’s an art that can impact lives, and changes the course of history. That’s why I love photography, and I would like to share my love with you for this art through this website that I created. In order to understand the importance of photography and its contribution to culture, I will share with you the work of two world-renowned photographers: Steve McCurry, and Jimmy Nelson. I will also talk about the different types of photography.
I hope reading about their work will get you inspired to take more pictures, and most importantly impact the world around you.

A brief history of photography

First picture ever taken

The first photo picture—as we know it—was taken in 1825 by a French inventor Joseph Nicéphore Niépce. It records a view from the window at Le Gras. Below is the first picture ever taken, for more info about the photographer click on the picture.

The first commercial camera

In 1900 the Kodak Brownie was introduced, becoming the first commercial camera in the market available for middle-class buyers. The camera only took black and white shots, but still was very popular due to its efficiency and ease of use.

Color photography

Color photography was explored throughout the 19th century, but didn't become truly commercially viable until the middle of the 20th century.
